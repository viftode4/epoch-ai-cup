# Question Strategy — Jury + Congress

## What to present strongly because it is defensible
- Real problem: wind energy and biodiversity share the same airspace.
- Radar classification is difficult because of noise, imbalance, and seasonal shift.
- System logic: movement + context + ranking-aware modeling -> uncertainty-aware decision support.
- Workflow: hypothesis -> experiment -> honest validation -> keep/reject.
- Key insight: shift and ranking mattered more than flashy tricks.
- Deployment framing: decision support, not blind automation.

## What to avoid overclaiming
- exact species certainty from radar
- fully autonomous mitigation
- universal generalization beyond the available competition context
- claims that every experiment helped

## Questions we should be ready for
- What exactly was your strongest result anchor?
- Why did you choose ranking-aware modeling?
- How do you handle uncertainty or ambiguous cases?
- What would you improve next with more data or time?
- Where would this fail in the real world?

## Best answer structure for almost every hard question
1. direct answer
2. concrete evidence
3. limitation
4. deployment consequence

## If research-copilot matters to the story
Only bring it in if it is one of these:
- the internal experimentation engine that made your workflow credible
- a demo-able artifact that proves your team builds more than models
- a support tool that helped you operationalize the research loop

If it is not central to the AI Cup system itself, keep it as a Q&A / team-capability point, not a core-slide point.

## What I still want to know about Research Copilot
To decide whether it belongs in the pitch or only in Q&A, I want 6 facts:
1. Was it actually used in your AI Cup workflow, or is it a separate project?
2. What is already working end-to-end today?
3. What is the strongest live demo-able action?
4. What pain does it solve better than notebooks + scripts + W&B?
5. Is it human-facing only, agent-facing only, or both in a real workflow?
6. Is it part of what makes your team special, or is it the actual product you want to pitch?
