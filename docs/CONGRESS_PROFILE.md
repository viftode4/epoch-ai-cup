# Congress Profile — Dutch AI Congress 2026 / AIC4NL

_Last updated: 2026-04-11_

## The real goal for us
At this congress, the presentation has to do more than score well with the jury.
It also has to make the **room** interested in:
- the team
- the research workflow
- the system
- the deployment potential
- follow-up conversations afterwards

So our congress positioning is:

> We are not just a model team. We are a research-driven team that built a deployable, honestly validated AI system for biodiversity-aware wind-energy decisions.

---

## 1) Verified event facts
- **Event:** The Dutch AI Congress 2026 / Het Nederlandse AI Congres 2026
- **Date:** **Tuesday, April 14, 2026**
- **Venue:** **DeFabrique, Utrecht**
- **Address:** **Westkanaaldijk 7, 3542 DA Utrecht**
- **Theme:** **Impact with AI, the Fair Tech way**
- **Scale:** AIC4NL says **more than 1,000** AI pioneers, entrepreneurs, policymakers, tech experts, and startups will attend.
- **Format:** plenary sessions, breakout sessions, AI Cup final, innovation floor, networking lunch, networking drinks.
- **Language:** main hall interpreted live from Dutch to English.
- **Travel:** ~15 min walk from Maarssen station, free shuttle buses, 700 parking spots, 14 EV chargers.

### Official sources
- https://aic4nl.nl/en/het-nederlandse-ai-congres-2026/
- https://aic4nl.nl/en/

## 2) Venue vibe profile
### Verified venue context
DeFabrique is an industrial-heritage venue with:
- multiple halls and modular spaces
- large event capacity
- movement between rooms and floors
- a strong visual / event-style atmosphere rather than a classroom setting

Source:
- https://www.defabrique.nl/locatie
- https://defabrique.nl/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/plattegrond-capaciteiten.pdf

### Likely vibe (**inference**)
The venue will probably feel:
- energetic
- busy
- modular
- social
- visual-first
- conversation-heavy
- louder and faster-paced than a normal academic room

### What that means
You do **not** win this venue with a dense technical lecture.
You win it with:
- a strong opening line
- a clean story
- a memorable system picture
- one or two sharp technical insights
- a team identity people remember

## 3) What the congress room likely wants
Based on the official theme, speakers, exhibitors, and breakout structure, the room is likely to reward:
- **impact**
- **responsible AI**
- **real deployment**
- **public-private value**
- **clear explanation**
- **systems thinking**
- **European / Dutch relevance**
- **credible teams, not just clever demos**

So the presentation should feel bigger than:
- a contest solution
- a Kaggle story
- a model benchmark

It should feel like:

> a strong example of how serious teams build trustworthy applied AI for real societal problems.

---

## 4) The best overall framing for the room
### Not enough
- “We trained a model and got a score.”
- “Here are the architectures we tried.”
- “Here is our leaderboard improvement.”

### Better
- “We tackled a real sustainability-versus-biodiversity problem.”
- “We built a disciplined research workflow, not just a one-off model.”
- “We learned that robustness under seasonal shift mattered more than flashy local gains.”
- “We turned that into a deployable decision-support system.”

### Best one-line identity
> We are a team that knows how to turn a hard real-world sensing problem into a rigorous, deployable AI system.

---

## 5) What makes the presentation interesting to the full congress
To make the room interested in you, the presentation needs to sell **four things at once**:

### 1. The problem is real
Wind energy and biodiversity share the same airspace.
That gives immediate relevance.

### 2. The team is serious
Your experimentation workflow is part of the value proposition:
- lots of testing
- honest validation
- rejection of weak ideas
- strong iteration discipline
- **award for most submissions** framed as disciplined learning

### 3. The system is smart
Enough technical juice to signal depth:
- radar trajectory + environmental context
- ranking-aware modeling
- cross-month robustness
- evidence-based architecture

### 4. The solution can matter beyond the competition
Deployment, human oversight, mitigation support, and practical usefulness.

---

## 6) What wins the venue specifically
### A. Strong first 20 seconds
The venue is likely high-attention-competition. People form an impression fast.

### B. One clean visual system story
A simple pipeline slide will beat five messy technical slides.

### C. One memorable insight
For example:
> The hardest part was not just classification, but generalizing under seasonal shift.

### D. One memorable team signal
For example:
> We built a disciplined experimentation pipeline and even won the award for most submissions.

### E. One memorable closing line
For example:
> The strength of our work is not only that it performs well, but that it is rigorous, ecologically grounded, and realistically deployable.

---

## 7) How to win the room, not just the jury
### The room should leave thinking:
- these people really understood the problem
- these people built something serious
- this could become something real
- I want to talk to them after

### To get that reaction, the presentation should emphasize:
- real-world stakes
- team workflow
- strong judgment
- clear system design
- practical implications

### Best presentation ratio
- **30%** problem + why it matters
- **25%** workflow / experimentation / what you learned
- **20%** system
- **15%** results / evidence
- **10%** deployment / impact / next step
